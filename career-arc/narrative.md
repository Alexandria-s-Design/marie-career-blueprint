# Dr. Marie Martin — Career Arc Narrative

## The Journey: From Jazz to Justice in Education

Marie Martin's path to educational leadership was never linear — and that is precisely what makes it powerful.

A graduate of USC's Thornton School of Music with a BA in Jazz, Marie began her career not in a classroom but on a stage. That early life as a performer taught her something no education textbook could: how to read a room, how to improvise when the plan falls apart, and how to make something beautiful out of collaboration.

## Phase 1: Practitioner (2000s)

Marie entered education through LAUSD as a substitute teacher — the front lines of public schooling in one of the nation's largest and most diverse districts. She quickly moved to full-time teaching, earning her CA Multiple Subject credential and eventually spending four years as a kindergarten teacher.

But the classroom was not enough. When an opportunity arose to teach internationally in Abu Dhabi, Marie and Charles took it. The experience of designing curriculum for students across cultures — Arabic, South Asian, Western — fundamentally shaped her understanding of what it means to teach with cultural intention.

## Phase 2: Designer (2010s)

Returning to the U.S., Marie pivoted to instructional design. At Albert Whitman, she designed learning experiences grounded in the same equity principles she had practiced in the classroom. At Microsoft's Program 11 initiative, she built training for underrepresented communities in tech. As an Instructional Systems Designer for the U.S. Army, she brought rigor and accessibility to military training.

Each role deepened her conviction: good instructional design is not neutral. It either reproduces inequity or disrupts it.

## Phase 3: Leader (2020s)

Marie earned her Ed.L. in Educational Leadership from USC — the same university where she had studied jazz decades earlier. Her doctoral work focused on equity in education, producing six publication-ready articles that bridge theory and practice.

She became CEO of Alexandria's Design, a woman-owned instructional design firm. She serves as PI on NSF STEM K-12 grants. She trains the next generation of teachers at UC Fullerton.

Three Teacher of the Year awards confirm what her students and colleagues already knew: Marie designs learning experiences that transform.

## Phase 4: Scholar-Practitioner (Now)

Marie is ready for the academy. Not as someone who left practice behind, but as someone who brings 20+ years of it through the door. Her model is Dr. Megan Bang — a scholar who built her academic career on the foundation of community-based work, bridging research and practice in ways that changed both.

Marie's target: Professor of Practice, Clinical Faculty, or Program Coordinator in Education, Equity, or Workforce Development. She brings:

- International teaching experience across cultures
- Instructional design across military, corporate, K-12, and higher ed
- NSF grant leadership as PI
- A publication pipeline grounded in her doctoral scholarship
- A woman-owned business that demonstrates entrepreneurial leadership
- A commitment to equity that is not theoretical — it is designed, built, and measured
